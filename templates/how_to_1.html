How To

Sites can support many different levels of making their information semantically valuable. Value Added News suggests first marking your content up using the hatom microformat.

hAtom provides semantic value for many aspects of a news story, but doesn't distinguish your content specifically as News. In order to do that, a site should support additional levels of semantic standards, we suggest you add hNews support. hNews (link to final specification) is an extension to the hAtom microformat developed in partnership with AP and released into the public domain.

Below is an example of a typical news article showing an example of how to integrate different levels of semantic value into your news articles. If you'd like help to add semantic value to your news stories, and are happy for us to use one of your stories as another example, email us at :

A typical news story

<a href="/">The Daily News</a>
</div>
<div class="story">
<h1>New investigation into Indian Plane Crash</h1>
<p class="by">By John Smith in Mumbai</p>
<p class="at">Published: 9.02am BST 15 Jun 2009</p>

<div class="figure">
<img src="20090519_plane.jpg" alt="Plane" />
<p>The runway at Sydney airport where the airplane was due to land. Photo: ADI</p>
</div>
<div>
<p>A new investigation has been launched into the plane crash in Mumbai earlier this year.</p>
<p>Questions were asked after the Skyline flight, which took off from Paris bound for Sydney on April 22nd, took an unscheduled detour over India.</p>
<p>Three hours into the second leg of the flight, at 12.30 Mumbai time, the pilot changed course.</p>
<p>Tom Reynolds, a spokesman for Skyline, said the company did not know why the flight veered off its usual route.</p>
<p>&quot;Pilots do not generally behave unpredictably&quot;, Reynolds told XYZ News, &quot;so it is very difficult for us to understand what happened to the B330 flight.&quot;</p>
<p>Jonathan Thompson, a civil aviation expert, thinks there is reason to believe the plane was hijacked.</p>
</div>

</div>
